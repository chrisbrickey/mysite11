My Story

I used to strategize about products and markets; now I build them.

  • User Experience & Strategic Programs

    Early in my career, I focused on customer needs and using technology to fill knowledge gaps. For a global manufacturer, I worked with medical devices, determining root cause of manufacturing and customer issues. In collaboration with biomedical engineers, I developed and implemented process manufacturing changes, strategic ventures, and new product development to improve the devices and the customer experience.

  • Strategy Consulting Across 3 Continents

    For over 4 years, I led consulting teams for large corporate clients in tech, e-commerce, and retail - to name a few. For one of the largest tech acquisitions of all time ($17B), I developed a research-based global brand strategy, which retained the customer base during transition. My client boosted revenue in subsequent months and years with co-branded products aligned to the strategy. Other notable projects include emerging market entry, valuation, CX, portfolio optimization, and data strategy.

  • Transition to Software Development

    I taught myself to code in Python, JavaScript, and Ruby. Then I couldn't stop - staying up all night building algorithms, games, and apps. I rounded out my full stack web developent skills by studying CS theory, pair programming with senior developers, and building and deploying more sophisticated web apps with React, Redux, Rails, Django, and PostgreSQL.

  • Software Engineer with Product Expertise

    At Ragtag, I collaborate with a team of engineers to build components of an API that is a national Call-To-Action aggregator. I'm also using test-driven development to build my own projects, like a CokeZero locator. I remain passionate about customer research and UX/UI but am especially interested in backend development. Hit me up if you're looking for a product-focused, full-stack engineer!

insta-étoile

photo-sharing and profile app for performing artists

insta-étoile is a full-stack (Rails5/React/Redux) web app inspired by Instagram. Danseur Étoile (literally 'star dancer' in French) is the highest rank within the Paris Opera Ballet. insta-étoile provides a visual platform to enable performing artists of all kinds to instantly share and showcase photos of their craft while building a community of supporters and followers. insta-étoile will further the careers of performing artists by broadcasting their experience in a beautiful and digestible format, linking robust immersive profiles with an artist's community and following.

Live link: www.insta-étoile.com

The files for this program are available at my github repository.

Space Race Game

a Python puzzle-solving and choose-your-own adventure game

I'm obsessed with space travel and revelled in creating this game about it. For now, players load the game from the command line and are prompted through a story. The story changes based on the player's input. The player must solve games of logic and chance in order to avoid a premature GAME OVER. Future development plans include a web-based interface.

The files for this program are available at my github repository.

Firework-Fun

JavaScript activity based on custom algorithms

This activity is set against a night sky and allows users to design their own fireworks show by choosing the location, color, and relative timing of fireworks. The explosion trajectories of the fireworks are generated by custom trigonometric algorithms.

Live link: www.firework-fun.com

The files for this program are available at my github repository.

GoTango

UX design and product management for a tango instruction app

I worked as both product manager and UX designer on this project. Learning tango is an ambitious undertaking, as I discovered when I travelled to Buenos Aires for that purpose. Dancing tango requires lots of practice, but the most common forms of practice - milongas and group classes - are often inconvenient and intimidating. GoTango uses mobile technology to make tango practice more easily a part of one's lifestyle - more spontaneous.

The files for this project are available at my github repository.
